Cavendish Cards

A calm, no-scoring deck for naming sensory and interaction needs. Point at a card, or lay a few — no reading needed to play.

No scoring. No points, no right answer, no winning. There is no wrong card and no wrong hand.

You choose what to share. Cards on the table stay face-down until you turn them up. Turning a card up is you sharing it.

Not a screening tool

This deck helps a person say what they need, on their own terms. It is not a way to assess, score, or sort anyone. A card names a need, never a symptom or a target.

Five ways to play
  • Show me — pick the one card that’s you right now.
  • Build my day — lay a place, a weather, and a couple of what-helps cards.
  • Class weather — everyone lays a weather card, and the room’s weather becomes visible. No one is named.
  • Map the edges — walk the lily-pad cards across a timetable to find the hard transitions.
  • Play as the environment — lay what-helps cards that conflict, and build one space that holds them all.
