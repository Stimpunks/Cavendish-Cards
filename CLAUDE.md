## What this is

Cavendish Cards is a free, open (CC0), neuroaffirming pictorial prompt-card deck by Stimpunks Foundation. A person of any age SHOWS how they feel and what they need by pointing at or laying pictures — **no reading required, no scoring, no matching, no right answer, no winning.** The person controls sharing: cards lie face-down by default, and turning one up *is* the act of consent. One card = one Markdown file; the deck grows in the open, community-authored. It comes from the Cavendish Space model and the ARLES design method.

It is **not** a screening/assessment tool and **not** an AAC board (see Decision log). A spread is a *design brief for the environment*, not a behavior report.

## Values (non-negotiable)

- **Broken systems, not broken people.** Counter-deficit throughout. A card names a need, a state, or a change to the environment — never the person as the problem.
- **Neurodiversity paradigm**, not pathology. Identity-first language; always **capitalize Autistic and Disabled**.
- **Human/community-made art only — no AI art.** Guidebook/Notes are human-authored.
- **Nothing about us without us.** Helen Edgar (Autistic Realms) owns the values-heavy and Autistic-community calls (see Collaborators for the current, looser review pattern).

## The realms (as of this snapshot — generators compute exact counts; check `cards/`)

- **Places** (~6): the cave, the campfire, the watering hole, the library, the habitat (+ your own). The five Cavendish zones. Fixed.
- **Weather** (~18): inner weather, the whole range good and hard. **Curated display order** (gentle range, no headings, no good/bad split).
- **What helps** (~28): the niche-construction realm; the largest on purpose. Grouped into ~12 sense **signposts** in its single-realm view (Being in charge, Sound, Light & looking, Touch, Pressure, Temperature, Movement, Mouth & nose, Space & enclosure, Telling & talking, People & time, Make your own).
- **Lily pads** (~10): transitions/moments. Renders in `web/app.js` as a normal browsable realm, same as any other family — the old always-on "moments strip" has been retired.
- **Growers** (~4): dandelion/tulip/orchid resilience metaphor. Optional.
- **Kind words** (~9): affirmations. App label **"Kind words"** (subtitle "Also called Love Locutions or Love Languages"); the **guidebook keeps "Love Locutions."** Reframed as **self-affirmation / self-advocacy** — **given to someone, or claimed for yourself**, never earned, never a reward. Folder/slug stays `love-locution`.
- **Interaction** (~6): the Autistic community's red/yellow/green communication badges (ANI/Autreat/Sinclair 1996). The one exception to face-down default — meant to be shown; has its own distinct card back.
- **Blank** (1): draw your own. Every realm also carries a `your-own` card.

## Card schema & conventions

- `cards/<realm>/<slug>.md`: `# name` (lowercase, deck-facing), `## Family` (folder-matching key), `## Image cue`, `## Prompt` (`—` for Kind words), optional `## Reflection` (per-card override), `## Notes` (guidebook entry — metaphor / what it names / how to hold; "When this card is out…"; **describe the card, never the person**), optional `## Pattern` (facilitator-layer Stimpunks Pattern map; **never card-facing** — stripped from `cards.json`), `----`, `## License` (CC0).
- **Notes carry a reframe, not just a label** — this is the anti-AAC feature. Credit lineage in Notes where relevant (Adkin & Gray-Hammond for monotropism terms; Nick Walker/NQLS for embodiment; Erin/@EisforErin for tendril theory; ANI/Sinclair 1996 for Interaction; Cavendish Space for intermittent collaboration).
- Voice: short declarative sentences, stakes first, no hedging, no marketing register, em-dashes and fragments for weight. US spelling. Deck-facing copy lowercase.
- Generators in `scripts/` read `cards/**` by folder. `build-all.py` runs them all. Reflection pools, display labels, signpost groups, curated order, subtitles, and guidebook realm-notes are constants at the top of `build-site.py`; the starter deck's curated order + play modes in `build-starter-deck.py`; the markdown guidebook's realm-notes in `build-guidebook.py`.
- Reflection questions: hybrid (per-realm pool in `build-site.py` + optional per-card `## Reflection` override). A spread **distributes** questions across the pool so they don't repeat.
- **Standalone web pages** generated by `build-site.py` into `web/` (all gitignored; Netlify rebuilds): `guidebook.html` + `implementation.html` from the cards; `why.html`, `origin.html`, `arles.html`, `example-spreads.html` from their `.md` sources via a small no-dependency `md_to_html`; `facilitator.html` by **reusing `build-facilitator-pdf.py`'s `parse()`** so the on-site sheet and the print PDF render from one source.
- **Paired constants kept in sync by hand:** `SCREENING` and `FRAMEWORKS`/`FRAMEWORKS_LEAD` appear in **both** `build-site.py` and `build-guidebook.py` and must match word-for-word (sync comment present). `SITE_NAV` in `build-site.py` is mirrored in hand-authored `web/index.html` (floating breakbar) — keep both in sync when adding a page. The Pattern-crosswalk slug registry lives in `build-guidebook.py`; `build-site.py` imports it.

### Build gotchas (still live)

- **Do not hoist the `weasyprint` import to module level in `build-facilitator-pdf.py`,** and do not change `parse()`'s `(title, kicker, blocks)` return shape — either one breaks the Netlify site build and weasyprint-less machines, because `build-site.py` reuses that `parse()` to render `facilitator.html`.
- **macOS PDF build** needs native libs (`brew install cairo pango gdk-pixbuf libffi`) plus a Homebrew-Python venv with `DYLD_FALLBACK_LIBRARY_PATH` set (Apple's system Python strips `DYLD_*` under SIP). The facilitator PDF and the print-and-play PDF only regenerate when their builders are run in that venv.

## Decision log (settled — don't relitigate without reason)

- **All ages, not just children** (per Helen). Framing broadened from child-specific: `child` → `person`; `adult` → `facilitator` for the support role, or age-neutral otherwise; the term of art is the **"facilitator layer"** (was "adult layer"). Self-use and peer-use are first-class. Child-safety commitments are retained and children are still named explicitly (e.g. the AAC-alongside line). `let me unmask` (What helps) is the resolved masking card: it names a niche-construction *need* (permission and a safe space to unmask), not a "you are masking" behavior-label. The deck never asks anyone to name masking as a meta-observation; `let me unmask` and `no-spotlight` address the masking *pressure*, not the mask itself.
- **Not an AAC board.** Inclusion test (CARD.md): a card earns its place if it names a hard-to-voice inner state or a niche-construction need AND carries a reframe — not if it's a want/object/action a person could request. "can't tell" (experience) belongs; "hungry" (request) does not. Sits **alongside** real AAC, never replacing it (child-safety line).
- **Not a screening tool** (README + guidebook).
- **Materials stay in the facilitator layer.** The deck names the *need*; specific materials (tents, projectors, budget hacks) live in the guidebook, the implementation layer (`cavendish-cards-implementation-layer.md`), and linked posts — not as cards.
- **Kind words reframe (resolved):** self-affirmation on the web, framed as **given to someone, or claimed for yourself** (never earned, never a reward). Per-card label is **"given or claimed"** (was "given, not read"); guidebook one-liner, starter-deck blurb, and BACKS section match. The distinct card back is retained but reframed to **"a kind word" / "you don't have to earn it"** (was "a gift" / "given, not earned") — anti-behaviorist stance as permission, not a verdict.
- **Distinct card backs = realms shown outside the face-down flow only.** The shared back is a privacy feature: face-down cards must be indistinguishable or a spread leaks before consent. So a distinct back is only safe for a realm that isn't laid face-down — **Kind words** (given/claimed) and **Interaction** (worn, shown face-up). Interaction's own back reads *how open i am* / *right now — show it, flip it*. The other five realms keep the shared back.
- **penguin pebbling** is the giving *verb*, not a card → guidebook note. **parallel existence** = body doubling → What helps.
- **Monotropic-spiral clinical depth** (burnout→psychosis) stays OUT of the person cards; "round and round" names only the everyday looping-thought feeling.
- **Seven play modes is the ceiling:** Show me · Build my day · Class weather · Map the edges · Play as the environment · Moving between · Build a niche.
- **Weather grouping:** flat with a curated order, NOT signpost headings (feelings don't sort cleanly; a good/bad split would betray the ethos).
- **Pattern crosswalk (facilitator layer).** A card may carry an optional `## Pattern` field mapping it to Stimpunks Pattern(s); renders in the Markdown + web guidebook only, never on the card face / starter deck / web card view (stripped from `cards.json`). Full map in `cavendish-cards-pattern-crosswalk.md`.
