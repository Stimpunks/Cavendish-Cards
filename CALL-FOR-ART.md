# Call for Art — Cavendish Cards

**Cavendish Cards** is a free, open deck that helps a person show how they feel and what they need — by pointing at a picture instead of having to find the words. It's made by and for neurodivergent and Disabled people, and it runs on one belief: **broken systems, not broken people.** A card names a need; it never labels a person.

Right now the deck works, but its pictures are plain placeholders. **We're looking for artists to draw the real thing** — and we'd love it to come from our community.

You make the art. We do all the technical work of fitting it onto the cards.

**There's no deadline — the call is open-ended.** Send art whenever you make it. Cards without a picture yet simply wait for the right illustrator, and the deck keeps growing as art comes in. You'll never miss a window; there isn't one to miss.

## The style we're looking for

The cards are for **all ages**, so the art should be:

- **Warm, gentle, and friendly** — calm rather than busy, never scary or clinical.
- **Simple and clear** — bold, readable shapes that still make sense at the size of a small playing card (about 2.5 inches wide).
- **Neuroaffirming and counter-deficit** — draw the *feeling or the metaphor*, not a "problem person." A *stormy* card is weather building, not a person in trouble; *stuck* is a calm pause on a lily pad, not a failure.
- **Inclusive** — where you draw people, draw a range of them: different skin tones, bodies, hair, and ages, and Disabled people simply present (ear defenders, mobility aids, AAC devices, and so on) as an ordinary part of the picture. Avoid stereotypes; don't gender things that don't need it.
- **Your own style** — crayon, paint, ink, collage, cut-paper, digital, whatever you love. The style is the point.

Please **don't** include any text (we add the words), any card border or frame (the card supplies that), or any copyrighted or brand characters. And please **no AI-generated images** — this deck's art is human- and community-made.

## What to draw

Each card comes with a short **image cue** — a plain description of the picture — plus the family it belongs to and a note on what it means.

- **Browse the deck:** https://cavendish.app/
- **Full list of cards, with every picture cue:** the [starter deck](./cavendish-cards-starter-deck.md)
- **What each card is *for*:** the guidebook on the site, or [`cavendish-cards-guidebook.md`](./cavendish-cards-guidebook.md) (helps you catch the feeling behind the cue)
- **About the project:** https://stimpunks.org/projects/cavendish-space-project/

Pick one card or a whole family — for example, *weather → buzzy* ("sparks, too many at once") or *places → the cave* ("a small den, low light, a person tucked in"). You don't have to claim cards in advance; just tell us what you're working on if you'd like to.

## Technical details

Don't worry about getting these exact — we resize, crop, and place everything. These just help your art fit nicely.

- **Shape:** the picture sits in a **near-square window** in the top two-thirds of the card — roughly **630 × 600 pixels** (just a touch wider than tall). Compose for a square-ish space.
- **The finished card** is 2.5 × 3.5 inches (750 × 1050 px at 300 dpi); we add the card's name and prompt underneath your picture.
- **Size:** if digital, aim for at least **1200 px on the longest side**. If it's on paper, **scan at 300 dpi** or photograph it flat in even light on white paper.
- **Background:** plain or transparent is best — we place it on a warm cream card.
- **Safe area:** keep the important parts a little in from the edges (the window has softly rounded corners and we may nudge the crop).
- **Format:** any of JPG, PNG, PDF, or SVG. Line art scans and vector files both work well; we'll clean up or vectorize as needed.

## How to submit

Email your art to **stimpunks@stimpunks.org**. Please include:

1. **Which card each file is for** — the family and card name (e.g. "what helps / headphones").
2. **How you'd like to be credited** — a name, handle, or link — or let us know if you'd rather stay anonymous.

That's it. We take care of cleanup, sizing, and adding the art to the cards.

## Licensing (please read)

Cavendish Cards is dedicated to the public domain under **[CC0 1.0](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/)** — free for anyone to use, print, and adapt. Art you submit will be released the same way. So please only send work that is **entirely your own** and that you're happy to share this freely. We're glad to credit contributors (CC0 doesn't require it, but we like to).
